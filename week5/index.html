The Role of State in GenAI Agents

LLMs are Stateless

Why State Management Matters

Maintain conversation context

Remember what was discussed previously

Track progress in workflows

Know which steps are completed

Remember facts & preferences

Store user information across sessions

Enable agent coordination

Multiple agents working together

‚öñÔ∏è Chains vs Agents

Two different approaches to solve problems with AI

üí°
Real-Life Analogy:

Chain = Recipe: Follow steps in order ‚Üí Guaranteed result

Agent = Chef: Decides what to cook based on ingredients ‚Üí Flexible result

‚õìÔ∏è üÜö ü§ñ

Let's understand the difference...

‚õìÔ∏è Chains: Fixed Workflows

Chain = Predefined sequence of steps (Step 1 ‚Üí Step 2 ‚Üí Step 3)

Example: Document Summarizer Chain
Read Document
‚Üí
Extract Key Points
‚Üí
Generate Summary
‚Üí
Format Output

Same steps every time, predictable result!

‚úÖ Advantages
  • Fast & predictable
  • Easy to debug
  • Lower cost
  • Reliable output
‚ùå Limitations
  • Can't adapt to changes
  • Fixed logic only
  • No decision making
  • Limited flexibility

ü§ñ Agents: Dynamic Decision Makers

Agent = Thinks and decides what to do next (Adaptive workflow)

Example: Research Agent

Task: "Research best laptops for gaming"

Agent decides:

  • Step 1: Search web for gaming laptops
  • Step 2: Found 10 results ‚Üí Decide to filter by budget
  • Step 3: Compare specs ‚Üí Decide to check reviews
  • Step 4: Analyze reviews ‚Üí Generate recommendation

Agent decides each step based on what it learns!

‚úÖ Advantages
  • Adaptive & flexible
  • Handles complexity
  • Smart decisions
  • Explores solutions
‚ö†Ô∏è Trade-offs
  • Slower execution
  • Higher costs
  • Less predictable
  • Harder to debug
